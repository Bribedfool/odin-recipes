Beer Battered Fish

Description

One of the main difficulties of cooking beer battered fish is finding a right balance of breading to fish ratio. This general recipe will produce a light and crispy breading. However, if you want the fish to be even lighter or perhaps thicker, then simply add less flour/pour more beer (for a lighter breading) or add more flour/pour more beer (for a thicker breading).

Make sure you also set aside at least 30 minutes for the batter to set before coating and frying the fish. This is usually to let the flour absorb the moisture (the beer and the egg), so that the mixture of the batter will be evenly distributed throughout. Also, make sure that your fish is dry as possible before coating and cooking. Otheriwse, the batter will slide off the fish. One trick to achieve this is to lightly coat the fish with flour. Another tidbit is the use of baking powder in this recipe. Baking powder activates and bubbles up when it contacts moisture (usually with an acid), as well as also activating when exposed to heat. This allows air bubbles to form in the batter which results in a lighter, airy breading.

As a side note, many baking powders are now doubly activated which means it becomes activate when either exposed to an acid or exposed to heat.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb. Cod
  • 3/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 Tbsp. paprika
  • 1/2 tsp. salt
  • 1/4 tsp. black pepper
  • 1 egg
  • 6 oz beer
  • 1/2 tsp. baking powder
  • Neutral cooking oil such as peanut, vegetable, canola, etc.

Steps

  1. Whisk together the flour, paprika, salt, pepper, egg, beer, and baking powder in a bowl.
  2. Cover the mixture and let rest for at least 30 minutes or up to 12 hours.
  3. Lightly season the fish with salt and pepper (not included in the ingredients).
  4. Heat oil to 365 degrees in a dutch oven, wok, or deep skillet of your choice. Make sure there is at least enough oil to fill the bottom of your pan.
  5. Dip a piece of fish in the batter and quickly transfer to the oiled pan.
  6. Cook until the fish is sufficiently browned on one side and flip. Around 4-5 minutes per side.
  7. Place fish on a cooling rack (lined on a baking sheet) to allow the excess oil to drain.
